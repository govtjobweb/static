RRB Assistant Loco Pilot (ALP) Recruitment 2026–27: 11,127 Assistant Loco Pilot Vacancies Approved

The Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) has approved 11,127 vacancies for the post of RRB Assistant Loco Pilot (ALP) for the 2026–27 recruitment cycle. This is expected to be one of the largest technical recruitment drives in Indian Railways in the coming year.

Final Update

According to the official information, the approval has been issued through Railway Board letter No. 2026(E/MPP)/25/13/ALP dated 19 February 2026. The vacancies will be filled through a Centralised Employment Notification (CEN) 2026, which will be released by the Railway Recruitment Board.

These posts will be distributed across 17 Zonal Railways and CLW (Chittaranjan Locomotive Works). Once the notification is released, eligible candidates will be able to apply online through the respective RRB websites.

The recruitment will provide a great opportunity for candidates who have completed 10th along with ITI or a Diploma in relevant trades. Since ALP is a technical position in Indian Railways, candidates with the required technical qualifications can apply and secure a stable government job with good salary and benefits.

RRB ALP 2026–27: Recruitment Overview

Official Railway Board Order

The Railway Board, New Delhi has reviewed the vacancy positions submitted by different Zonal Railways through the Indent Management Module of HRMS. After examining the requirements, the board has approved filling a large number of posts for the RRB Assistant Loco Pilot (ALP) position.

As per the order, the competent authority has given approval to release a Centralised Employment Notification (CEN) for the recruitment of 11,127 ALP posts across various Zonal Railways and Chittaranjan Locomotive Works (CLW).

📌 Important: At present, this is only an approval order issued by the Railway Board. The detailed recruitment notification (CEN 2026), which will include eligibility criteria, application dates, and the selection process, has not been released yet. Candidates will be able to apply only after the official notification is published by the Railway Recruitment Board (RRB).

RRB ALP 2026 Selection Process

The RRB Assistant Loco Pilot (ALP) Recruitment 2026 will follow a multi-stage selection process to shortlist eligible candidates. Applicants must clear each stage to move to the next phase of recruitment.

CBT 1 (Computer Based Test – Stage 1)

This is the first stage of the recruitment process and acts as a screening examination. It consists of objective-type questions. Candidates who qualify in CBT 1 will become eligible to appear for the next stage.

CBT 2 (Computer Based Test – Stage 2)

Candidates who clear CBT 1 will be called for CBT 2, which is a more advanced technical examination. This stage focuses on domain-related subjects and generally has a higher difficulty level.

CBAT (Computer-Based Aptitude Test)

The Computer-Based Aptitude Test is specifically conducted for RRB Assistant Loco Pilot posts. Performance in this test plays an important role as its marks are considered while preparing the final merit list.

Document Verification (DV)

Candidates who qualify in the previous stages will be invited for Document Verification, where their educational certificates, identity documents, and other eligibility proofs will be checked.

Medical Examination (ME)

Finally, candidates must pass the Railway medical examination, which follows strict medical standards required for safety-sensitive railway jobs.

Only candidates who successfully clear all stages of the selection process will be considered for final appointment as RRB Assistant Loco Pilots in Indian Railways.
